why choose us?

The UK is an attractive destination for international student who comes to pursue their education and also for individuals seeking employment opportunities. Recently, a rising number of students are looking for overseas education in order to build cosmopolitan social and cultural resources as well as upward social mobility. The country also offers employment opportunities for both EU and non-EU citizens, although the dynamics of work-related migration are influenced by factors such as economic conditions and policy changes. [1] These international student as well as employees needs assistance when they first move to the UK, with particular focus on the formal processes required. The web application aims to provide complete help to individuals who are moving to the UK by offering guidance and support in several areas. It is specifically designed to streamline and simplify procedures such as applying for a Biometric Residence Permit (BRP), registering with a General Practitioner (GP), applying for a National Insurance (NI) number, opening a bank account and dealing with Her Majesty's Revenue and Customs (HMRC) processes. The application's design and user experience will be prioritized to create an intuitive and user-friendly interface. Technologies and resources which will be used throughout the development cycle have been outlined.

Biometric Residence Permits: General Information for Overseas Applicants

This guidance offers detailed information about BRPs and Biometric Residence Cards (BRCs). BRPs and BRCs contain biographic and biometric details, indicating the holder's immigration status and entitlements while in the UK. They can be used to confirm identity when opening bank accounts, accessing public services, and demonstrating rights to study or work in the UK. Some categories of immigration may include the holder's National Insurance Number on the document

Biometric Residence Permits: General Information for Applicants, Employers, and Sponsors

An essential step for international students and employees moving to the UK is applying for a National Insurance (NI) number. The NI number is unique to each individual and is necessary for legal employment, claiming benefits, applying for a student loan, and paying voluntary National Insurance contributions. Applicants need to apply for an NI number if it is not already printed on their BRP and if they do not already possess one. The application process involves verifying one's identity and eligibility to work in the UK, and it is a crucial step for individuals looking to settle in the country and participate in various official processes
